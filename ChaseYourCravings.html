ChaseCheesy Mac Attack

Ingredients:

  • 16 oz elbow macaroni
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups milk
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 4 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the macaroni and cook according to the package directions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.
  4. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1-2 minutes, until the mixture turns golden.
  5. Slowly whisk in the milk, a little at a time, until fully combined.
  6. Add the salt and pepper, then bring the mixture to a boil, stirring constantly.
  7. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 2-3 minutes, until the mixture thickens.
  8. Stir in the shredded cheddar cheese and continue to stir until it has fully melted and the sauce is smooth.
  9. Add the cooked macaroni to the cheese sauce and stir until fully coated.
  10. Transfer the mixture to a large casserole dish.
  11. Sprinkle breadcrumbs over the top of the macaroni and cheese mixture.
  12. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the cheese is bubbly.
  13. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

ChaseSlow-Cooked Beefy Bliss

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds beef chuck roast, cut into chunks
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes, undrained
  • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 bay leaf

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Transfer the beef to a 6-quart slow cooker.
  4. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the same skillet and cook until softened, stirring occasionally.
  5. Add the beef broth, diced tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, paprika, salt, black pepper, dried oregano, cayenne pepper, and bay leaf to the slow cooker
  6. Stir everything until well combined.
  7. Cook on low for 8 hours, or on high for 4-5 hours, or until the beef is fork-tender.
  8. Discard the bay leaf.
  9. Serve hot over cooked rice, noodles, or mashed potatoes.

ChaseGolden Chicken Pot of Goodness

Ingredients:

  • 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into chunks
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 cup frozen corn
  • 1 cup carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric

Instructions:

  1. In a shallow dish, mix together the flour, salt, and black pepper.
  2. Coat the chicken chunks with the flour mixture.
  3. In a large Dutch oven, melt the butter over medium-high heat.
  4. Add the chicken chunks and cook until browned on all sides.
  5. Remove the chicken from the Dutch oven and set aside.
  6. Add the diced onion and minced garlic to the same Dutch oven and cook until softened, stirring occasionally.
  7. Add the chicken broth, milk, frozen peas, frozen corn, chopped carrots, chopped parsley, dried thyme, dried rosemary, dried sage, and ground turmeric to the Dutch oven.
  8. Stir everything until well combined.
  9. Add the browned chicken chunks back to the Dutch oven and stir everything until well combined.
  10. Bring the mixture to a boil over high heat, then reduce the heat to low.
  11. Cover the Dutch oven and let it simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  12. Serve hot, garnished with additional chopped parsley, if desired.

Chase Spicy Tikka Temptation

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 red bell pepper, seeded and cut into chunks
  • 1 green bell pepper, seeded and cut into chunks
  • Wooden skewers, soaked in water for 30 minutes before grilling

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the yogurt, oil, lemon juice, cumin, paprika, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, black pepper, garlic, and ginger.
  2. Add the chicken to the marinade and toss to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or overnight.
  3. Preheat a grill to medium-high heat.
  4. Thread the chicken, onion, and bell peppers onto the skewers.
  5. Grill the skewers, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are slightly charred, about 10-12 minutes.
  6. Serve the skewers over a bed of cooked basmati rice, garnished with cilantro leaves and lemon wedges.

ChaseFlavorful Pad Thai Adventure

Ingredients:

  • 8 oz pad Thai noodles
  • 1/2 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup roasted unsalted peanuts, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 3 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon chili flakes
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 2 limes, cut into wedges

Instructions:

  1. Soak the pad Thai noodles in warm water for 30 minutes, or until they are pliable but still firm. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the tamarind paste, fish sauce, brown sugar, and chili flakes until the sugar is dissolved. Set aside.
  3. Heat a wok or large frying pan over high heat. Add the oil and swirl to coat the pan. When the oil is hot, add the garlic and shrimp. Stir-fry until the shrimp are pink and cooked through, about 2-3 minutes.
  4. Push the shrimp to the sides of the wok, making a well in the center. Pour the beaten eggs into the well and let them cook for a few seconds, then scramble them with the shrimp and garlic.
  5. Add the noodles to the wok and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes, or until the noodles are heated through.
  6. Pour the tamarind mixture over the noodles and toss to coat.
  7. Add the bean sprouts and half of the chopped peanuts to the wok and stir-fry for another 1-2 minutes.
  8. Remove the wok from the heat and stir in the sliced green onions.
  9. Serve hot, garnished with the remaining chopped peanuts and lime wedges on the side.

ChaseVibrant Tacos with a Twist

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 package taco seasoning mix
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 8 to 10 taco shells
  • 1 can (16 oz) refried beans
  • 1 cup shredded lettuce
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup shredded cheese
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup sliced black olives
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Instructions:

  1. Brown the ground beef in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Drain the excess fat.
  2. Add the taco seasoning mix and water to the skillet, stirring until the beef is coated in the seasoning. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
  4. Warm the taco shells in the oven for 5 minutes, or until they are crispy and slightly golden
  5. Heat the refried beans in a small saucepan over medium heat.
  6. Assemble the tacos by spooning a layer of refried beans into each taco shell, followed by a layer of ground beef, shredded lettuce, diced tomatoes, shredded cheese, and sour cream.
  7. Garnish with sliced black olives and chopped cilantro.
  8. Serve with lime wedges on the side to squeeze over the tacos. Enjoy!

ChaseSavory Mediterranean Moussaka

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggplants
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp ground allspice
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F. Slice the eggplants into 1/4-inch rounds and place them in a single layer on a baking sheet. Brush both sides with olive oil and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Roast the eggplant in the oven for 20 minutes or until tender.
  2. While the eggplant is roasting, heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef, onion, and garlic, and cook until the beef is browned and the onions are soft.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, cinnamon, allspice, nutmeg, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Bring the mixture to a simmer and let it cook for 10-15 minutes until the sauce has thickened.
  4. To make the béchamel sauce, melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add the flour and stir constantly for 1-2 minutes to create a roux. Slowly whisk in the milk and continue to whisk until the sauce has thickened. Remove from heat and stir in the Parmesan cheese and beaten eggs.
  5. In a 9x13 inch baking dish, layer the eggplant slices in a single layer. Spoon the meat mixture over the eggplant and spread it out evenly. Repeat the layers until all the ingredients have been used, making sure to end with a layer of meat on top.
  6. Pour the béchamel sauce over the top of the moussaka and spread it out evenly. Bake in the oven for 45-50 minutes or until the top is golden brown and the moussaka is bubbling.
  7. Let the moussaka cool for 5-10 minutes before slicing and serving. Garnish with fresh parsley and enjoy!
